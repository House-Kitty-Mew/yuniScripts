# Simulated People Extension v2 — Full Design Specification

## 0. Core Philosophy

**Everything is AI-generated, not random.** Random number generators pick
*what kind* of event/personality/message happens, but the actual content
(text, descriptions, reasons, reactions, letters) is written live by the
DeepSeek API.  This gives every persona genuine depth — no two personas
ever send the same message, even in the same situation.

The system is a **living world simulation** with the Auction House at its
economic center.  Player actions (listing items, buying, cancelling) ripple
through this world and change persona behavior.

### 1.1 Regions (3+)
- `overworld` — Most personas live here. Subdivided by biome.
- `nether` — Harsh, dangerous. Special resources (ancient debris, glowstone).
- `end` — Isolated, mysterious. End-game resources (elytra, chorus, shulkers).
- `deep_dark` — Sub-region. Risk of Warden. Sculk-related resources.

### 1.2 Areas (generated per region)
Each region has 3-8 areas.  Each area has:
- **Name** (AI-generated, e.g. "The Verdant Reach", "Scorchfield Basin")
- **Biome type** (plains, forest, desert, swamp, mountains, etc.)
- **Resources** present (list of item IDs with rarity: common/uncommon/rare/unique)
- **Settlement type** (village, outpost, city, ruins, unclaimed wilderness)
- **Claimed by** (persona UUID or None)
- **Natural borders** (neighboring area IDs)

### 1.3 Territories
- An area can have 1-5 territories
- Each territory can be claimed by a persona
- Claiming costs money (from persona wallet)
- Claimed territories provide income (resource taxes)
- Unclaimed territories can be fought over
- Territory defense rating based on claimant's combat skill

### 1.4 Countries
- A persona who controls 3+ adjacent territories can form a **country**
- Country members get trade bonuses
- Wars can be declared between countries
- Country capitals generate unique local resources

## 2. Resource System

### 2.1 Resource Categories

| Category | Examples | Abundance | Regenerates? | Can be claimed? |
|----------|----------|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Common** | dirt, stone, wood, water | Everywhere | Yes (infinite) | No |
| **Uncommon** | iron, coal, copper, gold | Most areas | Slowly | Yes (vein) |
| **Rare** | diamond, lapis, redstone | Few areas | No | Yes (vein) |
| **Unique** | ancient debris, elytra, dragon egg | 1-3 per world | Never | Yes (claim) |
| **Infinite-Rare** | netherite, echo shards, heavy core | Everywhere but 0.01% chance per search | Technically infinite but effectively never found | No |

### 2.2 Resource Discovery
- Each simulation tick, active personas roll to discover resources in their area
- Discovery chance = `skill_mining * area_resource_rarity * AI_modifier`
- AI_modifier is generated by DeepSeek: "a tremor is felt near the northern ridge..."
- When discovered, the persona gets a one-time income boost
- Rare resources deplete the area's resource counter (finite resources)

### 2.3 Resource Claiming
- Personas can "claim" a resource vein they discovered
- Claim cost: `vein_value * 0.1` coins
- Claimed veins provide passive income each tick
- Other personas can challenge a claim (leading to conflict)
- Player items that match local resource shortages get bonus purchase priority

## 3. Skill System

### 3.1 Skills (1-100 each)

| Skill | Affects |
|-------|---------|
| **Mining** | Resource discovery chance, ore yield |
| **Combat** | Territory defense, war outcome, survival |
| **Farming** | Food income, crop yield |
| **Trading** | Purchase price negotiation (5-20% discount) |
| **Crafting** | Item quality when selling to AH |
| **Exploration** | Finding new areas, rare discoveries |
| **Leadership** | Territory cap, war morale, country stability |

### 3.2 Skill Progression
- Skills start at `random(10, 40)` based on archetype
- Skills increase by 0.5-2 per tick when relevant action is taken
- Mining: increases when persona "mines" (discovers resources)
- Combat: increases when persona fights
- Trading: increases when persona buys/sells on AH
- Skills cap at 100
- AI can award bonus skill points for exceptional events

### 3.3 Skill-Based Actions
- Personas decide what to do each tick based on their highest skills
- A miner with high mining skill will focus on resource discovery
- A warrior with high combat will patrol territories
- Decisions are AI-generated: "Grom Ironfoot heads to the mines today..."

## 4. Movement & Migration

### 4.1 Persona Location
- Every persona has a `current_area_id` in the database
- On creation, personas start in a random area matching their archetype
- Each tick, a persona may move to a neighboring area

### 4.2 Movement Reasons (AI-generated)
- "Seeking better mining prospects in the eastern mountains"
- "Fleeing the drought in the Verdant Reach"
- "Following rumors of a new trade route"
- "Escaping territory conflict"
- "Returning home after a successful expedition"

### 4.3 Migration Waves
- World events can cause mass migrations (e.g., "The Great Frost" causes
  overworld personas to move toward the nether)
- Migrations change regional supply/demand
- Player items in high-demand regions get price bonuses

## 5. Territory Wars & Conflicts

### 5.1 Conflict Types
| Type | Participants | Duration | Market Impact |
|------|-------------|----------|---------------|
| **Dispute** | 2 personas | 1-3 ticks | Minor price fluctuation on tools/weapons |
| **Raid** | 1 persona vs territory | 1-2 ticks | Medium demand for weapons/armor |
| **War** | 2+ countries/groups | 3-10 ticks | Major price changes, shortages |
| **Revolution** | Personas vs country leader | 5-15 ticks | Extreme volatility, refugee migration |

### 5.2 War Resolution
- Wars are resolved by: `total_combat_skill + territory_count + resources`
- Loser loses 1-2 territories (returned to unclaimed)
- Winner gains temporary income boost
- AI generates narrative: "The Battle of Sunken Pass ended with victory for..."

### 5.3 Market Effects
- Wars increase demand for: swords, armor, shields, food, arrows
- Wars decrease supply from: the affected region's resource output
- Post-war reconstruction increases demand for: wood, stone, glass, tools
- AI announces war events to players via broadcast

## 6. AI-Generated Message System

### 6.1 When Messages Are Sent
When a simulated persona buys an item from a *player's* listing, the AI
generates a personalized message that is sent to the player via tellraw.

### 6.2 Message Content (AI-generated based on persona profile)
- **Archetype personality** (merchant: formal/negotiating, vagabond: casual, warrior: blunt)
- **Current mood** (life events affect tone)
- **Relationship** to the item (urgent need, impulse, long-time want)
- **Any recent events** in their life

Example messages from the same purchase:
- **Merchant:** *"Good day! The emeralds have been transferred. Quality dirt
  — reminds me of the clay deposits near Sunken Pass. If you find more,
  do keep me in mind. — B. Goldvein"*
- **Warrior:** *"Dirt. Finally. My fort's walls are patched. Payment sent.
  — Gunnar Ironfoot"*
- **Mage:** *"Ah, excellent. The elemental essence in this dirt will serve
  my experiments well. A pleasure doing business. — Lyra Moonshadow"*
- **Vagabond:** *"Hey! Thanks for the dirt! Needed it for a... thing. Don't
  ask. Coins are yours. — Wren"*

### 6.3 Message Format
```
§7[AH] §f{persona_name} bought {count}x {item_id} for {price}em
§o§7"{message}"
```

The `§o§7` italicizes and grays the quote, making it clearly the persona's
own words.

## 8. AI Integration (DeepSeek API)

### 8.1 What AI Generates
| Item | Prompt Type | Frequency | Size |
|------|------------|-----------|------|
| World event creation | "Create a world event..." | ~5% of ticks | 100-200 chars |
| War narrative | "Describe this battle..." | Each war tick | 100-300 chars |
| Persona messages | "Write a message from..." | Each player purchase | 50-200 chars |
| Area descriptions | "Describe this region..." | On first access | 50-150 chars |
| Migration reasons | "Why would a miner move?" | Each movement | 50-100 chars |
| Resource discovery | "Describe finding..." | On resource find | 50-150 chars |
| Territory creation | "Name this territory..." | On claim | 20-50 chars |
| Market reports | "Summarize the economy" | Every cycle | 200-500 chars |

### 8.2 Cost Management
- Most AI calls use small models or short prompts
- Persona messages are batched: up to 5 per API call
- World events are generated in the main AI cycle
- The DeepSeek API key is the same one used by the main AH AI engine
- If API is unavailable, fall back to template-based generation with
  persona name/archetype substitution

### 8.3 Dynamic Content Caching
- Generated content (area names, descriptions) is cached in the DB
- AI only generates new content when needed (new area discovered, new war)
- Reuses cached content for repeat queries

## 9. Player Interaction Points

| Interaction | How it works | Feedback to Player |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Listing matches persona need** | Persona checks AH for needed items | `[needs: ⛏ N]` badge on listing |
| **Persona buys player item** | AI generates message | `§o§7"{message}"` in chat |
| **Territory war affects prices** | War changes regional demand | Broadcast announcement |
| **Resource discovery** | Persona finds something rare | May list on AH or send offer |
| **Player price matches persona memory** | Higher purchase chance | None shown directly |
